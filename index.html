Martin Luther King Jr.

Milestones

  • 1929: Born in Atlanta, Georgia
  • 1948: Graduates from Morehouse College
  • 1951: Earns doctorate from Boston University
  • 1955: Leads Montgomery Bus Boycott
  • 1963: Delivers "I Have a Dream" speech at March on Washington
  • 1964: Awarded Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1968: Assassinated in Memphis, Tennessee

Education

King attended Morehouse College, a historically Black college, where he was deeply influenced by the social justice teachings of sociologist George Kelsey.

He went on to earn his doctorate in systematic theology from Boston University, focusing on the concept of social justice in Christianity.

Hobbies

King enjoyed playing music, including the violin and piano. He also loved reading, particularly the works of philosophers and theologians.

He was known for his passion for baseball and often used sports metaphors in his speeches and writings.
